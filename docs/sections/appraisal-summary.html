Section 19: Appraisal Summary

Written by the appraiser, this section summarises the appraisal discussion across the four GMC Good Medical Practice domains plus a general summary.

Section name: Section_19_AppraisalSummary
Fluent accessor: XfaPdf::appraisalSummary()
Navigation label: Summary of the appraisal discussion

What's in this section?

FieldTypeWhat it is
generalTextareaGeneral summary of the appraisal discussion
knowledgeTextareaSummary of knowledge, skills and performance
safetyTextareaSummary of safety and quality
communicationTextareaSummary of communication, partnership and teamwork
trustTextareaSummary of maintaining trust

All five fields are multiline textareas. There are no repeatable items or conditional fields in this section. The four domain fields (knowledge, safety, communication, trust) correspond to the four domains of the GMC's Good Medical Practice framework.

Setup

Before filling any fields, load the PDF in your controller:

use Xfa\Pdf\Facades\XfaPdf;

// Load the PDF file
XfaPdf::setFile(storage_path('app/xfa-pdf/appraisal.pdf'));

Reading existing data

Read all fields in this section at once:

// Read everything in Appraisal Summary
$data = XfaPdf::read('Section_19_AppraisalSummary');

// Or using the fluent accessor
$data = XfaPdf::appraisalSummary()->read();

// $data looks like:
// [
//     'general' => 'This was a thorough and reflective appraisal discussion...',
//     'knowledge' => 'Dr Mitchell demonstrated strong clinical knowledge...',
//     'safety' => 'Dr Mitchell has shown a clear commitment to patient safety...',
//     'communication' => 'Feedback from colleagues and patients consistently highlights...',
//     'trust' => 'Dr Mitchell maintains high standards of probity and professional behaviour...',
// ]

Read a single field:

$general = XfaPdf::appraisalSummary()->readField('general');
// 'This was a thorough and reflective appraisal discussion...'

$knowledge = XfaPdf::appraisalSummary()->readField('knowledge');
// 'Dr Mitchell demonstrated strong clinical knowledge...'

Summary Fields

Each field is a standalone multiline textarea written by the appraiser. The fields map to the GMC Good Medical Practice domains, with an additional general summary at the top.

General summary

Field: general

An overall summary of the appraisal discussion, covering the key themes and any overarching observations by the appraiser.

XfaPdf::appraisalSummary()->updateField(
    'general',
    'This was a thorough and reflective appraisal discussion. Dr Mitchell '
    . 'presented a well-organised portfolio with clear evidence across all '
    . 'four domains of Good Medical Practice. She has made excellent progress '
    . 'against last year\'s PDP and has identified appropriate new objectives '
    . 'for the coming year. Her commitment to quality improvement is '
    . 'particularly noteworthy, with three completed projects demonstrating '
    . 'measurable patient benefit. There are no concerns to escalate.'
);

Knowledge, skills and performance

Field: knowledge

Summary of evidence relating to Domain 1 of Good Medical Practice: maintaining and developing knowledge, skills, and performance.

XfaPdf::appraisalSummary()->updateField(
    'knowledge',
    'Dr Mitchell demonstrated strong clinical knowledge with 52 hours of '
    . 'CPD activity, well above the minimum requirement. Her completion of '
    . 'the Advanced Life Support recertification and the RCGP diabetes '
    . 'management course show a commitment to maintaining core clinical '
    . 'skills. The diabetes audit demonstrated the application of new '
    . 'knowledge to practice, resulting in improved HbA1c outcomes for her '
    . 'patient cohort. Her plan to develop musculoskeletal medicine skills '
    . 'through supervised clinics is an appropriate stretch goal.'
);

Safety and quality

Field: safety

Summary of evidence relating to Domain 2: maintaining patient safety and improving the quality of care.

XfaPdf::appraisalSummary()->updateField(
    'safety',
    'Dr Mitchell has shown a clear commitment to patient safety through '
    . 'her quality improvement activities. The falls pathway project '
    . 'reduced unnecessary hospital admissions by 12% and the repeat '
    . 'prescription workflow improvement reduced processing delays from '
    . '72 hours to 24 hours. She was involved in one significant event '
    . 'during the year, which was handled appropriately with a thorough '
    . 'reflection demonstrating learning and changes to practice. No '
    . 'patient safety concerns were identified.'
);

Communication, partnership and teamwork

Field: communication

Summary of evidence relating to Domain 3: communicating effectively, working collaboratively, and maintaining partnerships.

XfaPdf::appraisalSummary()->updateField(
    'communication',
    'Feedback from colleagues and patients consistently highlights '
    . 'Dr Mitchell\'s excellent communication skills. Her colleague '
    . 'feedback scores were above the national mean in all categories, '
    . 'with particular strengths noted in team leadership and '
    . 'approachability. Patient feedback was overwhelmingly positive, '
    . 'with high satisfaction scores for consultation manner and '
    . 'explanation of conditions. The falls pathway project demonstrated '
    . 'effective multi-disciplinary working across organisational '
    . 'boundaries with A&E, physiotherapy, and community nursing teams.'
);

Maintaining trust

Field: trust

Summary of evidence relating to Domain 4: maintaining trust through probity, professional behaviour, and health declarations.

XfaPdf::appraisalSummary()->updateField(
    'trust',
    'Dr Mitchell maintains high standards of probity and professional '
    . 'behaviour. She confirmed compliance with all probity requirements '
    . 'and had no complaints or fitness to practise concerns during the '
    . 'appraisal period. She declared no health issues affecting her '
    . 'ability to practise safely. She has appropriate indemnity cover '
    . 'and her GMC registration is up to date. There are no concerns '
    . 'regarding maintaining trust.'
);

Complete Controller Example

Here's a full example filling the entire Appraisal Summary section in a Laravel controller:

use Xfa\Pdf\Facades\XfaPdf;

class AppraisalController extends Controller
{
    public function fillAppraisalSummary()
    {
        $pdfPath = storage_path('app/xfa-pdf/appraisal.pdf');

        // -----------------------------------------------
        // 1. Fill all summary fields
        // -----------------------------------------------
        XfaPdf::setFile($pdfPath);

        XfaPdf::appraisalSummary()->update([
            'general' =>
                'This was a thorough and reflective appraisal discussion. '
                . 'Dr Mitchell presented a well-organised portfolio with '
                . 'clear evidence across all four domains of Good Medical '
                . 'Practice. She has made excellent progress against last '
                . 'year\'s PDP and has identified appropriate new objectives '
                . 'for the coming year. Her commitment to quality improvement '
                . 'is particularly noteworthy. There are no concerns to '
                . 'escalate.',

            'knowledge' =>
                'Dr Mitchell demonstrated strong clinical knowledge with '
                . '52 hours of CPD activity, well above the minimum '
                . 'requirement. Her completion of ALS recertification and '
                . 'the RCGP diabetes management course show a commitment to '
                . 'maintaining core clinical skills. The diabetes audit '
                . 'demonstrated application of new knowledge to practice, '
                . 'resulting in improved patient outcomes.',

            'safety' =>
                'Dr Mitchell has shown a clear commitment to patient safety '
                . 'through her quality improvement activities. The falls '
                . 'pathway project reduced unnecessary hospital admissions '
                . 'by 12%. She was involved in one significant event which '
                . 'was handled appropriately with thorough reflection. No '
                . 'patient safety concerns were identified.',

            'communication' =>
                'Feedback from colleagues and patients consistently '
                . 'highlights Dr Mitchell\'s excellent communication skills. '
                . 'Colleague feedback scores were above the national mean '
                . 'in all categories. Patient feedback was overwhelmingly '
                . 'positive. The falls pathway project demonstrated '
                . 'effective multi-disciplinary working across organisational '
                . 'boundaries.',

            'trust' =>
                'Dr Mitchell maintains high standards of probity and '
                . 'professional behaviour. She confirmed compliance with all '
                . 'probity requirements and had no complaints or fitness to '
                . 'practise concerns. She declared no health issues affecting '
                . 'her ability to practise. Appropriate indemnity cover is '
                . 'in place and GMC registration is up to date.',
        ]);

        // -----------------------------------------------
        // 2. Save the PDF
        // -----------------------------------------------
        XfaPdf::save();

        return redirect()->back()->with('success', 'Appraisal Summary saved.');
    }
}

Quick Reference

What you want to doCode

Read one field XfaPdf::appraisalSummary()->readField('general')
Update one field XfaPdf::appraisalSummary()->updateField('knowledge', 'text...')
Update multiple fields XfaPdf::appraisalSummary()->update(['field' => 'value', ...])
Set general summary XfaPdf::appraisalSummary()->updateField('general', 'text...')
Set knowledge summary XfaPdf::appraisalSummary()->updateField('knowledge', 'text...')
Set safety summary XfaPdf::appraisalSummary()->updateField('safety', 'text...')
Set communication summary XfaPdf::appraisalSummary()->updateField('communication', 'text...')
Set trust summary XfaPdf::appraisalSummary()->updateField('trust', 'text...')
Save changes XfaPdf::save() or $manager->saveXfaPdf($xfaPdf)
